# Glossary

This glossary contains key terms and definitions related to Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics.

## A

**Actuator**: A component of a robot that converts energy (typically electrical) into physical motion.

**Autonomous System**: A system that can operate independently without human intervention.

## B

**Behavior Trees**: A mathematical model of action sequences that describes the behavior of a robot or AI agent as a tree structure.

## C

**Computer Vision**: A field of artificial intelligence that trains computers to interpret and understand the visual world.

## D

**Deep Learning**: A subset of machine learning based on artificial neural networks with representation learning.

**Dexterity**: The capability of a robot to perform fine motor tasks with precision.

## E

**Embodied AI**: Artificial intelligence that is integrated into a physical form and interacts with the real world.

**Ethical AI**: AI systems designed and deployed with consideration for ethical principles and human values.

## G

**Gazebo**: A 3D simulation environment that provides accurate physics simulation for robotics.

## H

**Human-Robot Interaction (HRI)**: The study of interactions between humans and robots.

## I

**Isaac ROS**: NVIDIA's collection of hardware-accelerated perception and navigation packages for ROS 2.

**Isaac Sim**: NVIDIA's high-fidelity simulation environment built on the Omniverse platform.

## L

**Learning from Demonstration**: A technique where robots learn tasks by observing human demonstrations.

## N

**Navigation Stack**: A collection of software packages that implement robot path planning and navigation.

## P

**Perception**: The ability of a robot to interpret and understand its environment through sensors.

**Physical AI**: AI systems that interact with and operate in the physical world through robotic platforms.

## R

**Robot Operating System (ROS)**: A flexible framework for writing robot software that provides hardware abstraction, device drivers, and useful software libraries.

**ROS 2**: The second generation of the Robot Operating System with improved architecture for production use.

## S

**Simulation**: The creation of an artificial environment to model real-world scenarios for testing and development.

## V

**Vision Language Actions (VLA)**: Multimodal neural networks that combine visual perception, language understanding, and action generation.

**VR/AR**: Virtual Reality and Augmented Reality technologies used in robotics simulation and human-robot interaction.
